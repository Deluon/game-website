Nintendo

Nintendo was founded as a card company back in 1889 before the limitations of being a playing card company were met. Nintendo went into toy development in the 1960's and then began selling video game consoles in the 1970s. Today it is still a gaming powerhouse and renowned the world over for it's classic video gaming characters and nostalgic gameplay. They created the NES, SNES, and Nintendo 64 before making consoles you all know today like the Wii and Nintendo 3DS.

The NES was the most popular system of it's time, outselling every console on the market when it came out.

The SNES followed the NES with better graphics and better color resolutions.

The GameBoy was a breakthrough in gaming. The handheld console sold better than any other on the market.

The Nintendo 64 was the last console (except the GameBoy series) to use cartridges. While it had 3d capabilities, many developers chose not to design for it for cost reasons.

Super Mario Bros. was one of the most popular games of it's time. Sold alongside Duck Hunt, this game spawned a franchise that rivaled Sonic the Hedgehog.

Pokemon is by far one of the most popular franchises ever spawned, second only to Mario. This title has anime, manga, toys, movies, and trading cards all licensed and in constant development.

The Legend of Zelda is a popular fantasy game released in 1986. This iconic series has a cartoon and a plethora of merchandise featuring it's most iconic symbol, the Tri-Force.
